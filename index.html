01Understanding EBPg. 01

You are not in the
AI business. You are
in the clarity business.

Enterprises and individuals don't come to you because they're curious about AI. They come because they're anxious about it — and you make the anxiety disappear.

Ekhlaque Bari helps organisations and people become genuinely AI-first. Not by chasing tools, but by changing how they approach a task. The philosophy is disarmingly simple and rare: task first, tool later. You teach tech to non-tech people and refuse to hide behind jargon. That restraint is the brand.

The work shows up as live, instructor-led, interactive workshops, masterclasses and consultancy — and as a steady stream of thought leadership that builds authority over time. The product is not a video library. It is you, in a room, making something complicated feel obvious.

A website for this cannot behave like a tooling startup. It has to behave like a thinker. The job is trust, and trust is built through clarity, restraint and story — not feature lists.

02Why a new website, why nowPg. 02

The current site sells
workshops. It doesn't
sell you.

There is a gap between how good the work is and how the website makes it feel.

01

It looks like a template, not a thinker

The present site is a capable WordPress build, but its visual language is generic. For a man whose entire pitch is taste, judgement and clarity, a templated look quietly undermines the message before a word is read.

02

It leads with services, not the idea

Visitors meet a menu — masterclass, workshops, consultancy, recruitment — before they meet the philosophy that makes any of it worth buying. The most persuasive thing you own is introduced last, or not at all.

03

There is no story, so there is no memory

Information is present; narrative is absent. Nothing escorts a visitor from "who is this" to "I trust this person" to "I want them in my organisation." Without a story, nothing is remembered and nothing is forwarded.

04

It contradicts the core message

"Task first, tool later" is a philosophy of restraint. A busy, effect-heavy site says the opposite. The medium should prove the message — calm, deliberate, confident.

05The big ideaPg. 05

The organising concept for the entire site

The Quiet
Authority.

The website behaves the way Ekhlaque behaves in a room: it removes noise, slows everything down, says one thing at a time — and by the end, you trust it completely.

What it means in practice

Every page earns attention before it asks for anything. We open on a single, confident statement — not a menu. We let white space and silence do the persuading. We reveal the offer only after the visitor believes in the person. The drama comes from pacing: long calm passages, then one decisive moment.

Why it wins

It is the only concept that makes the medium prove the message. "Task first, tool later" becomes something the visitor feels in the scroll, not something they read in a paragraph. It is also unmistakably ownable — no competitor in AI education is brave enough to be this restrained.

06Design directionPg. 06

A visual language
built from subtraction.

A direction we will refine with you in week two. The principles below are the spine; the surface gets art-directed together.

01

Type does the heavy lifting

A dramatic serif paired with a precise grotesque, plus a monospace for structure and data. Headlines run large and editorial; serif italics carry emphasis and humanity — the new.studio register, made yours.

02

Two tones, no escape hatch

Ink and paper, with a single accent used at under two percent of the surface — a scalpel, not a paintbrush. The discipline itself signals taste.

03

Drama through pace, not effects

Full-bleed inversions, long quiet passages, then one hard contrast moment. Motion narrates the scroll; nothing moves for decoration.

04

Grid you can feel

A visible editorial grid, hairline rules, tabular numerals, generous margins. The structure is part of the aesthetic — like a well-set book.

07Experience & storytellingPg. 07

The homepage,
told as a story.

Seven scenes. Each one moves the visitor from stranger to believer. Nothing asks for anything until the trust is earned.

The Statement

A single, full-screen sentence. No menu noise, no carousel. One idea, set huge, that frames everything: you teach thinking, not tools. The visitor decides in three seconds that this is serious.

The Belief

The philosophy, slowed right down. "Task first, tool later" delivered as a short, paced manifesto — the turn where curiosity becomes respect.

The Person

Ekhlaque, made monumental. Who he is, why non-tech people trust him, what he has changed. Authority without a CV dump.

The Proof

The room itself — live workshops, the way sessions feel, the outcomes. Story-led testimonials and enterprise results, not a logo soup.

The Offer

Only now: masterclass, workshops, consultancy. Introduced as paths into the same philosophy — clear, calm, easy to choose between.

The Thinking

A thought-leadership surface — writing, talks, ideas — that compounds authority and gives the site a reason to be revisited.

The Invitation

One decisive call: start a conversation. After six scenes of earned trust, the ask feels obvious rather than pushy.

10The processPg. 10

Five weeks,
four moves.

P1

Discover & Define

We align on the truth of the brand, audit what exists, and lock the narrative spine, message hierarchy and sitemap. We leave this phase agreeing on the story before anyone touches a pixel.

P2

Direct

Two design directions on the homepage and key moments, plus the content narrative. You pick one. We resolve type, palette, motion principles and the system foundations.

P3

Design

We design every page and state to final fidelity, including responsive and motion intent, across two structured review rounds. Sign-off here is the gate to build.

P4

Build & Launch

A faithful Framer build with CMS, motion and QA — then content load, training, go-live and a fortnight of post-launch support.

14Terms & next stepsPg. 14

The fine print,
kept honest.

Timeline assumptions

The 5-week schedule assumes a single decision-maker, feedback within 2 working days at each gate, and content/assets supplied by end of Week 2. Delays in either shift the timeline by the equivalent duration.

Revisions

Two structured revision rounds are included at the design stage. Further rounds or scope changes are estimated and approved before work proceeds.

Content

Final copy, logos and any media are provided by EB. We provide narrative structure, headline direction and first-draft copy support — not full long-form copywriting.

Platform

Delivered on Framer under EB's own Framer workspace and plan. Goldenflitch hands over full ownership, an editing guide and a recorded training session.
